About

I moved from clinical nutrition into product because a nutritionist helps one person at a time, and a product helps everyone who uses it. That trade has shaped every role since.

The science background still does daily work. A BSc in Human Nutrition and Physiology means I can sit between clinicians, engineers and commercial teams and translate between them. In health tech, where a wrong clinical detail costs user trust (or worse), not many PMs can do that.

I operate best with high autonomy and ambiguity: sole product functions, unplanned acquisitions, platforms built for one market being adapted for another. Give me clarity on the priority and the constraint, and I run with it.

"AI products don't fail because the model isn't good enough. They fail because they don't fit the workflow, there's no clinical rigour, or nobody defined success before shipping."

I hold that view because I've shipped three of them.
